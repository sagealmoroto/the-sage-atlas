The Partier

“Alive where the music never ends”

At a Glance

  • Plans travel around nightlife, concerts, festivals, and club culture
  • Enjoys high-energy destinations with strong entertainment scenes
  • Could be a gambler, raver, bar-crawler, or all of the above
  • Most alive in social, expressive, or celebratory environments
  • More active than the Relaxer, more extroverted than the Explorer
  • Related tourism types: Nightlife tourism, event tourism, festival tourism, gambling tourism, bachelor/bachelorette tourism, music tourism

Who They Are

The Partier doesn’t travel to slow down — they travel to turn up. Whether they’re dancing on a beach at sunrise, hopping between rooftop bars, or catching a once-in-a-lifetime music festival, their best travel stories start after dark. While not all Partiers party the same way, they share one thing in common: they seek places that feel alive. Loud, energetic, celebratory — that’s where The Partier feels most at home, even far from home. Their perfect trip is more about rhythm than rest.

What They Want to Do

From full moon parties and neon-lit nightclubs to casino floors, street parades, and after-hours lounges, The Partier is wherever the action is. They may fly across the world for a specific event — Carnival in Brazil, Yacht Week in Croatia, Pride in Tel Aviv — or simply gravitate toward cities that never sleep. Some are bar-hoppers, others are concert junkies, and some love the glitz of VIP lounges and all-night glamour. Regardless of the scene, The Partier seeks connection, movement, and celebration.

Why It Matters

For The Partier, nightlife isn’t just about drinking or dancing — it’s about energy, expression, and experiencing joy through togetherness. Travel is their way of meeting new people, letting go of routine, and living fully in the present. They don’t just want to see a destination; they want to feel its pulse. Whether it’s on a dance floor, a rooftop, or a crowded festival street, their most memorable moments come when the music’s loud and the lights are low.
