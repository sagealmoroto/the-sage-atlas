WanderType

Read below to find out more about the various vacation personality types.

The Classics

The Tourist

Loves iconic landmarks, souvenirs, and city tours.

→

“If I'm going to another country, I want to see what made it so famous in the first place!”

Behaviors:

  • Prioritizes major monuments, landmarks, and museums
  • Prefers tried-and-true itineraries with minimal surprises

Notable:
This is the traveler who wants the highlight reel.

The Culture-Seeker

Passionate about history, art, and local customs.

→

“I want to feel the soul of the place, not just see it.”

Behaviors:

  • Seeks out traditions, festivals, and spiritual practices
  • Prefers small towns, rural areas, and living culture over crowds

Notable:
Culture-seekers aren't just history buffs — they’re meaning-seekers.

The Foodie

Explores new places through taste and texture.

→

“If you want to understand a culture, eat its food.”

Behaviors:

  • Prioritizes food tours, street food, and local restaurants
  • Builds entire itineraries around iconic dishes

Notable:
The exploration of food is what makes the trip worthwhile.

The Bold

The Adventurer

Seeks thrills, action, and adrenaline in nature.

→

“Let’s climb it, jump off it, or ride through it.”

Behaviors:

  • Builds trips around adrenaline-fueled experiences
  • Feels most alive in rugged, intense landscapes

Notable:
They don’t just want thrills — they want to feel tested, alive, and in motion.

The Explorer

Fueled by curiosity and the unknown.

→

“I want to understand the world — not just see it.”

Behaviors:

  • Seeks out remote or under-the-radar locations
  • Favors immersive journeys over bucket-list stops

Notable:
Explorers crave discovery — of landscapes, cultures, and themselves.

The Partier

Lives for nightlife, energy, and connection.

→

“Why go if we’re not gonna go out?”

Behaviors:

  • Plans travel around festivals, raves, and nightlife districts
  • Chooses destinations based on vibe, energy, and social scenes

Notable:
The Partier lives for the moment — and wants that moment to be unforgettable.

The Rechargers

The Relaxer

Values stillness, leisure, and slowing down.

→

“Let me unplug, slow down, and just be.”

Behaviors:

  • Travels to rest, not do
  • Favors tranquil destinations like lakes, hot springs, and scenic drives

Notable:
The Relaxer finds joy in slowness, comfort, and quiet beauty.

The Wellness Devotee

Seeks healing, mindfulness, and inner balance.

→

“I travel to reconnect with myself.”

Behaviors:

  • Seeks retreats, wellness rituals, and spiritual practices
  • Chooses destinations based on their emotional or energetic pull

Notable:
The Wellness Devotee isn’t just chasing peace — they’re finding presence.

The Viewfinders

The Photographer

Captures the world one frame at a time.

→

“I want to capture the moment that makes you feel something.”

Behaviors:

  • Chooses destinations based on visual interest or contrast
  • Travels with a camera or sharp eye for beauty

Notable:
Photographers are emotional explorers — their souvenirs are moments made permanent.

The Naturalist

Feels most alive surrounded by nature.

→

“I feel most alive when I’m in nature.”

Behaviors:

  • Plans travel around wildlife, ecosystems, and natural wonders
  • Chooses hikes, national parks, and eco-conscious stays

Notable:
The Naturalist doesn’t just want to see nature — they want to belong to it.
